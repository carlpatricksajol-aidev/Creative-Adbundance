One structure, three renders

Identical art-director prompt and the same template + product references, run through three renderers. The point of the test: is the quality about the platform, or the prompt structure?

The takeaway. All three are simple, on-brand, and show the real pouch in its actual moment of use. None invented a jar, none cluttered the canvas. The renderer barely mattered.

The quality came from the prompt structure: template for composition only, product form locked to the photo, one concrete use-scene, brand as a light skin, copy capped at one headline plus one line plus one CTA. That is the step to bake into the pipeline.
